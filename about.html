IEEE KPRIET EMBS SBC (IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society Student Branch Chapter at KPR Institute of Engineering and Technology) stands out as an innovative hub for biomedical engineering enthusiasts. The chapter empowers aspiring engineers with hands-on workshops, high-impact technical events, and collaborative projects that foster cutting-edge biomedical innovation and technical skill development

Vision and Mission

IEEE KPRIET EMBS SBC is built to inspire and enable budding engineers, equipping them with 21st-century skills such as creativity, critical thinking, collaboration, and communication. The chapter provides an environment for showcasing technical talents and contributing to transformative biomedical solutions.

Impact and Collaboration

By bridging classroom theory with real-world applications, the chapter prepares graduates to tackle global health challenges. Collaborative partnerships include societies like BMESI and leaders across the biomedical industry, giving students diverse experiences for professional growth.

Why Join IEEE KPRIET IEEE SBC?

  • Participate in impactful workshops, symposiums, and national events.
  • Collaborate with peers, industry experts, and researchers.
  • Gain certification and recognition for technical achievements.
  • Access a future-focused engineering journey rich with opportunities and innovation.
  • Transform healthcare through technical excellence and community engagement.

Major Signature Events(2024-2025)

  • IGNUZ’24 National Symposium: A three-day symposium featuring hackathons, design challenges, technical posters, and inclusive engineering workshops for special children.
  • IEEE EMBS Sponsored Workshop on Calibration of Critical Care Devices: A focused hands-on experience with medical equipment calibration.

Recent & Regular Event Highlights

  • Reverse Engineering in Biomedical Devices: Hands-on ventilator workshop covering device assembly, troubleshooting, and certification.
  • IEEE Day 2024 Celebrations: Multiple tech events—hackathons, paper presentations, codeathons, innovation pitching, panel talks, and career guidance.
  • WIE Day 2025 – Women in Engineering: Panel discussions and networking to celebrate gender diversity in technology.
  • Cloud Computing Webinar: Recent developments in cloud tech delivered by industry experts.
  • STEMTHEM Experiential Program: STEM outreach camps for younger students.
  • Networking with LinkedIn – Webinar: Professional development session on career networking in collaboration with IEEE Power & Energy Society.

Scale and Recognition

  • Events routinely host 100+ participants and receive grant support (e.g., $1000+ for biomedical innovation).
  • Received $5,300 EPICS in IEEE grant for “Interactive Hand-Eye Coordination Trainer for Special Children” awarded to our advisor Dr. Allwyn Gnanadas for outstanding innovation in inclusive biomedical technology.
  • Strong collaborations with industry and academia for expert-driven learning.
  • Partnerships with IEEE education partners and national societies.

Ongoing Initiatives

  • Regular webinars on emerging topics (IoT, Cloud, AI in Healthcare).
  • Alumni success talks and IEEE Impact Creator sessions.
  • Faculty development and first-year engineering orientation.
  • Student paper contests, design challenges, and tech competitions.

With a multifaceted event calendar, IEEE KPRIET EMBS SBC stands at the forefront of biomedical engineering innovation, learning, networking, and professional growth in the academic community.
